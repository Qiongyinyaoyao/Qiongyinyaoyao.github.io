Judy-----How to improve your academic English

Posted on 2020-05-28

Academic style

1)making tentative statements

2)objective

3)use of passive voice

4) nominalizations

5)formality

6)discipline-specific vocabulary

Essay structure and layout

1.Introduction—–introduce the reader to your topic

a)The Hook——to draw the reader into reading the rest of the essay ( eg:
According to XXX )

b)The background information——mention the background

c)The thesis——mention the main idea of the essay and state your position
on the issue

2.Body paragraph (5-8sentences)

a)Topic sentence——introduce the main idea of the paragraph

b)The argument (4-6sentences)——state and explain the argument. The
sentences should be clear and in logical order

c)The conclusion sentence—– to restate and wrap up the paragraph

3.Conclusion

a)The transition sentence——this sentence needs to restate the
issue/topic you are giving about and your opinion

b)Summarize the arguments—–restate your 3 main arguments in 2-3 sentences

c)Restate your thesis—–wrap up your essay with one strong sentence that
is your thesis re-worded

Appropriate use of citations and references

citation—–In the essay, just indicate the surname of the author/journal
(indicate “ unknown “if the author is unknown) and the date of publication of
the book/journal/website

1.do not just use one citation at the end of the paragraph

2.provide citation when you change ideas

3)use citations to support your own ideas

4)take advantage of your own writing to include citations

[Quotation is to quote directly or indirectly what someone else has said.
Citation is to describe what someone else has said in one’s own words.

But both have to be quoted, quotation is to indicate where the sentence is on
that page, and citation is to indicate where it came from.]

reference—– After the essay, more detailed information should be included
(such as :author’s surname, initial years, title of book, edition of number,
where published, publisher…)

1)use a standard format, such as Harvard Style

2)are listed at the end of the essay

3)are sorted by alphabetical order
